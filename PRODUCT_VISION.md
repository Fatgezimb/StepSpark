# Product Vision

## Product Name

StepSpark

## Mission

Create the world's best USMLE Step 1 visual learning platform focused on Instant Recall Cards, NBME-style question generation, rapid recognition, and long-term retention.

## Learner Promise

StepSpark should help students recognize high-yield Step 1 patterns quickly, explain why an answer is correct, reject tempting distractors, and retain core concepts over time.

## Product Pillars

1. **Instant Recall Cards** - compact visual prompts that train fast recognition of tested concepts.
2. **NBME-Style Question Generation** - rigorous clinical vignette questions with high-quality distractors and explanations.
3. **Visual Learning** - diagrams, annotated images, flowcharts, tables, and memory anchors that reduce cognitive friction.
4. **Retention** - spaced repetition, review scheduling, mastery tracking, and adaptive reinforcement.
5. **Speed** - every workflow should support rapid practice, rapid feedback, and rapid correction.
6. **Trust** - medical accuracy, citations, and review workflows are product requirements, not optional polish.

## Target Users

- USMLE Step 1 students
- Medical students in pre-clinical and dedicated study periods
- Tutors and educators building high-yield learning sets
- Future institutional users who need measurable retention and performance insights

## Product Principles

- Visual first, but never decorative at the expense of learning.
- Every learning object must have a clear cognitive job.
- Questions must test reasoning, not trivia alone.
- Explanations must teach the rule and the trap.
- The system must prefer durable retention over short-term streak mechanics.
- Medical content must be source-aware and reviewable.

## Non-Goals For Phase 0

- No application UI.
- No user accounts.
- No question-generation engine.
- No production content database.
- No monetization system.
- No medical claims beyond product planning language.
